    LET'S SAVE THE EARTH

    Why This Project?

    Energy conservation is one of the most effective ways to combat climate change and ensure a sustainable future. Excessive energy use leads to:

    • Increased Carbon Emissions: Burning fossil fuels releases CO₂, contributing to global warming.
    • Global Warming & Climate Change: Rising temperatures cause extreme weather events and habitat loss.
    • Resource Depletion: Overuse of non-renewable energy sources affects future generations.
    • High Energy Costs: More energy usage leads to expensive electricity bills.

    Our goal is to educate and encourage energy-efficient habits. Whether it is switching to LED bulbs, using public transport, or optimizing home energy use, every small action counts.

    Water Waste: Facts/Impacts

    Trillions of Gallons Wasted

    The U.S. alone wastes about 1 trillion gallons of water annually due to leaks, inefficient use, and wasteful habits.

    Shower Power… or Water Devour?

    Did you know that a 30-minute shower can waste 63 gallons of water—more than what some families in developing countries use in an entire week?

    Climate Change & Water Scarcity

    Rising temperatures increase evaporation and droughts, making fresh water even more precious. By 2025, half of the world’s population may live in water-stressed areas.

    How to Reduce Water Waste

    Fix leaks – A dripping faucet can waste 3,000 gallons per year! Turn off the tap while brushing teeth – Saves up to 8 gallons per day. Use water-efficient appliances – Look for EPA WaterSense labels.

    Also, with growing water shortages around the world, shortening your shower by just 5 minutes can save over 3,800 gallons of water per year, helping to preserve this vital resource for future generations.
